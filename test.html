Lover of dogs and japanese stationery

I am a visual designer with a focus on UX/UI and graphic design, based in the San Francisco Bay Area. I create with the love of helping others and making a positive impact. I admire dogs for their loyalty, compassion, and support; thus I design so as to satisfy stakeholders, empathize with users, and provide effective and efficient experiences.

When offline, I am most likely to be found exploring a city, drooling over chocolate chip cookies, or expressing myself through movement and wiggles, otherwise known as dance, during odd hours of the night/morning/day. Eep.

Projects

Hippo Time

Worked with a team of undergraduate students and faculty from the University of California, Irvine to integrate biofeedback into a video game, to teach children with autism spectrum disorder proper deep breathing techniques to deal with everyday stressors.

As a student programmer, I helped program individual scenes in Unity to incorporate in the Hippo Time game. I also took part in recording and keeping track of progress made on the video game using Bitbucket and TeamGantt.

CHRS Brochure

As a graphic designer for the Center for Hydrometeorology and Remote Sensing at the University of California, Irvine, I helped to design graphics and visual layouts for the department brochure.

A preview of the department brochure may be found here.

ICS Website Redesign

Worked to redesign the website for the University of California, Irvine Donald Bren School of Information and Computer Sciences with a small team of undergraduate students as well as the Director of Communications for the school. Particpated in agile sprints to elicit requirements, produce personas and scenarios, create low- and high-fidelity mockups, and generate a live working prototype. The main goal was to create an updated and modern design as well as to move the website onto WordPress.
