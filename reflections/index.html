User Interviews

Before even starting to design my portfolio, I conducted user interviews as a way to kind of elicit requirements. I was able to interview a diverse audience, including undergraduate computer science students, a product designer, an informatics professor, and the Director of Communications for the Donald Bren School of Information and Computer Sciences at the University of Claifornia, Irvine. Having a diverse audience helped me to figure out what different types of users would expect from my portfolio. The qualities that stood out the most to me were uniqueness, storytelling, and personality. Thus I aimed to incorporate them in my own portfolio.

Competitor Analysis

As a second step, I completed a competitor analysis document in which I looked at different visual portfolios and user interface/experience company websites to observe what kinds of features they included. This allowed me to realize how important my aesthetic and color scheme would be in my own portfolio.

Feature Value Matrix

Completing a feature value matrix allowed me to jot down what exactly I would use and include in my portfolio, along with details in how I would implement everything. I also estimated the difficulty and worth of implementing each feature.

Feature Prioritization

Prioritizing which features I would include in my portfolio based on my feature value matrix helped me to take the process of actually building my portfolio one step at a time. I focused first on features with high priority, then on features with lower and lower priority. I also generated a Kano model to help visualize a graphical version of my prioritization.
